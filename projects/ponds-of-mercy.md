# Ponds of Mercy (Urban Wetlands - BWA & BNG)

## Overview

The Ponds of Mercy project (referencing a historical 13th-century Chinese term for urban lakes and wetlands) was a baseline ecological and socio-economic assessment of two major urban wetlands in the Western Province of Sri Lanka: the Bellanwila-Attidiya (BWA) Sanctuary and the Bandaragama (BNG) Wetlands. 

The project sought to assess the impact of rapid urbanization on wetland hydrology, water quality, and biodiversity, and to promote sustainable co-existence models.

## Core Elements

- Seasonal biodiversity surveys (capturing wet, dry, and migratory bird seasons).
- Participatory Rural Appraisal (PRA) and Rapid Rural Appraisal (RRA) interviews.
- Historical comparison of faunal records to map biodiversity decline.
- Stakeholder mapping (municipal departments, local community, and NGOs).

## Contribution Focus

- Serving as primary investigator and presenter at the Sri Lanka Association for Fisheries and Aquatic Resources (SLAFAR) sessions.
- Conducting RRA community interviews and drafting community maps.
- Analyzing historical faunal trends against newly collected field data.

## Timeline

- **August 2005 – December 2005** (BWA field survey: 5 months, 10–15 field days per month; BNG field survey: Dec 6–24, 2005; presented at SLAFAR in June 2008)

## Budget

- **$700,000 USD** (corrected to 2026 USD; representing the total programmatic allocation for the Wetland Conservation Project [WCP] and UNESCO baseline surveys, of which this project was an integrated urban research component, funded internally by SLWCS and executed with volunteer research assistance)



## Impacts & Outcomes

- **BWA Survey**: Conducted **79 RRA interviews** and mapped threats across 3 Grama Niladhari Divisions (GNDs), identifying major pressures from landfilling, weed infestation, and industrial wastewater.
- **BNG Survey**: Mapped 5 GNDs across 2 Divisional Secretary Divisions (DSDs), documenting severe human impacts from illegal slaughterhouse effluent dumping, bird poaching, and land reclamation.
- **Historical Analysis**: Compiled a comparative database comparing the 2005 survey with historical data (from 1988, 1992, and 1994) to quantitatively demonstrate the decline in urban wetland biodiversity.
- **Strategic Policy**: Provided baseline data and zoning proposals to local municipal councils to advocate for the protection of urban wetlands.
