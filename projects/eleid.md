# eleID (AI-Supported Elephant Identification)

## Overview

eleID refers to technical work on automated elephant recognition and monitoring support.

## Intended Use

- Improve monitoring continuity
- Support collaborative habitat and elephant tracking workflows
- Contribute to data consistency in long-term conservation programs

## Timeline

- **2006 – 2010** (volunteer team design in 2006–2007; official grant work launched in May 2009; Beta system presented in January 2010)

## Budget

- **$300,000 USD** (corrected to 2026 USD; sponsored by the Google.org Geo Challenge Grant and administered through the Tides Foundation under grant agreement `TFR09-01213`)


## Impacts & Outcomes

- **AI-Powered Recognition**: Implemented a C++/OpenCV computer vision algorithm based on Principal Component Analysis (PCA/Eigenfaces) to identify individual elephants from their frontal face profile.
- **Accuracy Rate**: Achieved a tested identification rate of **68% - 74%** using standard database images.
- **Collaborative Geo-Portal**: Designed `www.AsianElephant.info`, an interactive web portal integrated with the Google Earth API. It allows the public and experts to upload photos, input relative features (tail length, ear folds), and geo-reference elephant sightings.
- **Population Analytics**: Enabled non-invasive tracking of elephant ranges, migration patterns, and herd counts using scientific mark-and-recapture methods.

## Awards & Recognition

- Recipient of the **2009 Google.org Geo Challenge Grant Award** to support mapping and conservation solutions.
