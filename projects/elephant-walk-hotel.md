# Elephant Walk-Through Hotel Concept & Escape Tourism Initiative

## Project Overview

The **Elephant Walk-Through Hotel (Elephant Walk Thru)** is an innovative ecotourism lodge and wildlife corridor model conceived by the Sri Lanka Wildlife Conservation Society (SLWCS). The project was designed as a pioneering social enterprise model to resolve human-elephant conflict (HEC). By establishing an ethical eco-lodge directly along natural elephant migration corridors, the initiative combined habitat preservation with high-end "escape tourism" and local employment, proving that community-based hospitality could generate self-sustaining revenues to fund local conservation activities.

The project served as the basis for a published, peer-reviewed business case study widely used in global university MBA and entrepreneurship curricula to study nonprofit strategy, environmental scanning, and social entrepreneurship.

---

## Role & Key Contributions

*   **Case Study Co-Author**: Collaborated with academic researchers to document the strategic planning, HEC challenges, and commercial business model, co-authoring the peer-reviewed case study published by **Ivey Publishing** (distributed globally by **Harvard Business Publishing**).
*   **Operational & Strategic Design**: Formulated the operational parameters, community benefit-sharing models, and land-use integration plans for the walk-through lodge concept.
*   **Escape Tourism Logistics**: Managed and directed field guiding, logistics, and location services for international ecotourists, dignitaries, and high-profile media crews (including National Geographic and Discovery Channel), validating the ecotourism revenue model.

## Impacts & Outcomes

*   **Global Academic Contribution**: Co-authored the published business case **"Elephant Walk Thru" (Ivey Publishing Product #904M52)**, distributed by **Harvard Business Publishing**, establishing a standard reference for social enterprise and environmental scanning.
*   **Ecotourism Innovation**: Designed a replicable commercial blueprint for conservation-linked hospitality, proving that wildlife corridor preservation could be funded through premium travel experiences.
*   **Media & Brand Recognition**: Managed logistics and location guiding for major television productions (National Geographic, Discovery Channel), generating significant PR, awareness, and direct local revenue for SLWCS.
*   **Livelihood Diversification**: Provided direct employment for local tracking guides, drivers, and hospitality staff, aligning community incentives with elephant protection.
