# Turtle Conservation Project

## Overview

The East Coast Turtle Conservation Project was a community-integrated initiative designed to protect sea turtles and their nesting habitats along the Eastern Province coastline. 

The project sought to address high levels of poaching, by-catch mortality, and predator disturbance through community beach patrols, nesting protection, and alternative livelihood incentives.

## Core Elements

- Systematic night surveys and beach monitoring.
- In-situ nest protection (protective cages and nesting area security).
- Community education, capacity building, and awareness programs.
- Alternative income generation (composting centers, plant nurseries, and sustainable agriculture).

## Contribution Focus

- Designing field survey procedures and protocols.
- Negotiating military and national security clearances for night beach patrols.
- Consolidating survey data and preparing grant applications (USFWS Sea Turtle Conservation Fund and SOSF).
- Leading stakeholder workshops in fishing villages.

## Timeline

- **2008 – 2010** (preliminary surveys launched in September 2008; night beach surveys conducted Dec 2008 – Jan 2009; proposed project execution period July 2009 – July 2010)

## Budget

- **$600,000 USD** (corrected to 2026 USD; representing the total budgeted program target for the USFWS and Save Our Seas Foundation proposals)


## Impacts & Outcomes

- **Threat Identification**: Identified and quantified the primary threats to nesting populations, noting high levels of egg poaching and nest predation by wild boars.
- **Nesting Baseline**: Documented nesting occurrences of Endangered Olive Ridley turtles at Peanut Farm and Arugam Bay.
- **Security Collaboration**: Secured necessary security clearances from the national security forces to conduct research patrols in previously restricted areas.
- **Alternative Livelihoods**: Drafted co-management plans to establish a wetland plant nursery and organic composting center to provide alternative employment for local poachers.
