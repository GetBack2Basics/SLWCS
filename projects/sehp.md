# Saving Elephants by Helping People (SEHP)

## Overview

SEHP is a community-integrated human-elephant conflict (HEC) mitigation initiative designed to reduce conflict risk while improving local livelihoods and conservation outcomes.

## Core Elements

- Community engagement and co-design
- Field-level conflict mitigation interventions
- Applied monitoring and adaptive management
- Integration of conservation and development priorities

## Contribution Focus

- Program implementation and coordination
- Technical support and multi-stakeholder alignment
- Presentation and dissemination of lessons learned in local and international forums

## Timeline

- **1997 – Present** (Ongoing)

## Budget

- **$6,000,000 USD** (corrected to 2026 USD; funded across multiple phases by CIDA Local Tsunami Recovery Fund, USFWS, Born Free Foundation, and SLWCS HEC programmatic allocations)



## Impacts & Outcomes

- **HEC Mitigation**: Initiated and maintained Sri Lanka's longest continuously operating solar-powered electric fences.
- **Agricultural Enhancement**: Increased crop yields in buffer zone communities by **92%** by eliminating elephant crop-raiding losses.
- **Livelihood Development**: Benefited approximately **165,000 individuals** across the Central, North Central, and Eastern Provinces.
- **Project Orange Elephant**: Introduced crop diversification (e.g., planting citrus trees which elephants naturally avoid) to establish alternative income streams and biological barriers.

## Awards & Recognition

- Recipient organization of the **2008 UNDP Equator Initiative Prize** for outstanding community-based poverty reduction and biodiversity conservation.
- Winner of the **2009 Whitley Fund for Nature Associate Award**.
