# Sri Lanka Fire Department Project (Emergency Fire Response System)

## Overview

The Sri Lanka Fire Department Project (EFRS) was a collaborative GIS development effort to design, build, and deploy an automated Emergency Fire Response System (EFRS) centerline database and mapping portal for the Colombo metropolitan area. 

The project was executed by the Center for Conservation GIS (c2GIS) in partnership with All One River GIS Services LLC and GeoXMF.

## Core Elements

- Road network centerline digitization and attribution (road classes, street names).
- Geolocation mapping of Points of Interest (POIs) such as fire hydrants, water sources, fire stations, and hospitals.
- Spatial analysis for closest response features using Euclidean distance workflows.
- Professional training for municipal fire service personnel.

## Contribution Focus

- Georeferencing satellite and map vector data.
- Hand-digitizing road networks using tablet PCs and converting datasets into vector formats.
- Coordinating with the Colombo Fire Brigade and municipal telecom providers.
- Delivering training workshops for local operators.

## Timeline

- **October 2008 – December 2008** (Phase 1 prototype development and personnel training)

## Budget

- **$200,000 USD** (corrected to 2026 USD; funded and executed under the c2GIS capability center and All One River GIS partnership)


## Impacts & Outcomes

- **Centerline Mapping**: Successfully developed a centerline database and routing baseline for a **50 sq. km. project area** in Colombo.
- **POI Database**: Collected and cataloged GPS locations of critical EFRS response points (hydrants, stations, and water bodies).
- **Municipal Capacity**: Conducted **30 hours of system training** and supervised 60 hours of independent work for a team of **4 Fire Department officers**, enabling local database maintenance.
- **Future Roadmap**: Formulated needs assessments and systems requirements for a provincial and nationwide 119/911-linked emergency response roll-out.
