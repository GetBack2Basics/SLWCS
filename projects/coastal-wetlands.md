# Coastal Wetland Complexes (KKP & SAU)

## Overview

The Coastal Wetland Complexes project involved comprehensive socio-ecological and biodiversity baseline surveys of the Kalpitiya-Karaitive-Puttalam (KKP) and Seruvila-Allia-Ullakalie (SAU) coastal wetlands. 

The project integrated GIS remote sensing analysis with Rapid Rural Appraisals (RRA) to study human impacts (such as aquaculture and commercial fisheries by-catch) and recommend sustainable management strategies.

## Core Elements

- Multi-taxa biodiversity inventories (point-count and random line transect methods).
- GIS mapping of land use, mangrove cover change, and water security points.
- Rapid Rural Appraisals (RRA) and socio-economic profiling of coastal communities.
- Assessing local awareness of environmental legislation and enforcement.

## Contribution Focus

- Serving as primary investigator and presenter at the Sri Lanka Association for Fisheries and Aquatic Resources (SLAFAR) scientific sessions.
- Conducting remote sensing analysis and GIS baseline mapping.
- Designing and administering socio-economic survey templates.

## Timeline

## Impacts & Outcomes

- **Biodiversity Cataloging**: Documented rich coastal faunal diversity:
  - **KKP**: 185 bird, 181 plant, 49 butterfly, 26 reptile, 10 mammal, 8 dragonfly, and 6 amphibian species (including the threatened endemic Sri Lanka Wood Frog *Rana gracilis*).
  - **SAU**: 123 bird, 57 butterfly, 11 amphibian, and 4 reptile species.
- **Threat Assessment**: Documented a **63% decline (approximately 993 hectares) in mangrove cover** in the Puttalam area between 1981 and 1992, highlighting the destructive impact of commercial shrimp aquaculture.
- **Fisheries By-Catch**: Assessed the ecological cost of local commercial push-nets and by-catch mortalities of marine turtles.
- **Community Profiling**: Surveyed coastal communities' education, service access, and livelihoods to develop balanced conservation incentives.
