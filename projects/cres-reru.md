# CRES / RERU Methodologies

## Overview

CRES and RERU methodologies were used to structure rapid, objective evaluation of ecological and socio-economic priorities in conservation landscapes.

## Application Areas

- Protected and proposed protected areas
- Wetlands and coastal zones
- Community-conservation interface planning

## Typical Outputs

- Priority ranking and site assessment support
- Baseline reporting to inform policy and project planning
- GIS-linked evidence for technical recommendations

## Timeline

- **2005 – 2008**

## Budget

- **$200,000 USD** (corrected to 2026 USD; funded through specific programmatic allocations, including IUCN Netherlands Committee and UNESCO-Government of Sri Lanka roundtable projects)


## Impacts & Outcomes

- **Protected Area Management**: Structuring and completing the socio-economic baseline survey for the Flood Plains National Park (FPNP), providing crucial data for park management plans.
- **UNESCO Heritage Evaluation**: Supported zoning and planning recommendations for the proposed Seruwila UNESCO World Heritage Site and the Lunugala Division of the Badulla District.
- **Methodological Integration**: Established a rapid assessment model that combines Participatory Rural Appraisal (PRA) with remote sensing and GIS to provide spatial evidence for policy decisions.
