# Conservation GIS Center (c2GIS)

## Overview

The c2GIS initiative established a technical GIS capacity hub within SLWCS to support conservation planning, analysis, mapping, and reporting.

## Strategic Value

- Built internal technical capability for landscape-level conservation planning
- Supported project design, donor reporting, and evidence-based interventions
- Enabled data-backed decision support across biodiversity and socio-economic contexts

## Noted Outcomes (from source material)

- Developed as a self-financing technical unit
- Supported acquisition and use of GIS tools, spatial datasets, and field-linked workflows

## Timeline

- **2006 – Present** (Ongoing)

## Budget

- **$1,100,000 USD** (corrected to 2026 USD; representing the aggregate value of GIS infrastructure, training capacity, and satellite imagery, including SPOT imagery secured via Planet Action and software/training grants from SCGIS and CIDA)


## Impacts & Outcomes

- **Mapping Scale**: Conducted baseline mapping and resource utilization surveys spanning over **30% of Sri Lanka's Divisional Secretariat Divisions (DSDs)**.
- **International Support**: Assisted the Forest Department of Bengkulu Province in Sumatra, Indonesia, by providing GIS capacity development and training.
- **UNESCO World Heritage Nominations**: Supported the Government of Sri Lanka and UNESCO by compiling GIS-based baseline and resource utilization surveys for the proposed Seruwila and Lunugala heritage sites.
- **Capacity Building**: Hosted national and regional Conservation GIS courses and training programs (such as the SCGIS short course in 2008).

## Awards & Recognition

- Recipient of the **2007 SCGIS (Society for Conservation GIS) Software and Training Grant** which provided core GIS applications and professional training.
