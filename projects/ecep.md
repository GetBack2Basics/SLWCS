# Environment Conservation Education Program (ECEP)

## Overview

ECEP (also referred to as the Outdoor Environmental Education Program - OEEP) is an interactive, experiential educational initiative designed to engage students in rural schools near national parks. It aims to raise awareness of biodiversity, local conservation challenges, and human-wildlife co-existence.

## Core Elements

- Outdoor and field-based experiential learning curricula.
- Establishment and capacity development of School Environmental Societies.
- Training teachers and select students as local environmental resource leaders.
- Production and distribution of educational materials on biodiversity in local languages.

## Contribution Focus

- Curriculum design and training manual preparation.
- Coordinating field-based study sessions for students.
- Facilitating collaboration between schools, the Ministry of Education, and SLWCS staff.

## Timeline

- **2009 – 2010**

## Budget

- **$400,000 USD** (corrected to 2026 USD; comprising UNDP/GEF/SGP grant, Canadian International Development Agency [CIDA] co-financing, and in-kind support from SLWCS)


## Impacts & Outcomes

- **Target Reach**: Conducted pilot sessions for school children aged 11–16 years across **20 schools** in the Lahugala, Hullanuge, and Panama divisions (Ampara District).
- **Curriculum and Materials**: Distributed custom outdoor learning material covering birds, butterflies, turtles, and HEC mitigation strategies.
- **Co-Education Extension**: Expanded program offerings to include Tamil-medium students in the Pottuvil Divisional Secretary Division (DSD).
- **Leadership Capacity**: Enabled students and teachers to independently lead environmental conservation actions through school-level clubs.
